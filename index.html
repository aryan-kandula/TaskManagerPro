How to Use It
1
Import Your Canvas Assignments
This is the fastest way to get all your coursework in. On your PC, open Canvas and click the Calendar icon in the left sidebar. On the Calendar page, look to the right side of the screen where your course list appears under Calendars. Scroll to the bottom of that panel — you'll find a small link that says Calendar Feed. Click it, copy the URL that appears, then come back here, click ⬇ Import, paste it into the Canvas Calendar Feed URL field, and hit Import & Watch. Every assignment across all your courses will load automatically.
2
Connect Google Calendar (Optional)
In Google Calendar, open Settings → select a calendar → scroll to Integrate Calendar and copy the Secret address in iCal format. Paste it into the Google Calendar iCal URL field in the Import panel. Personal events will be imported and grouped under a separate Google Calendar bucket, keeping them distinct from your course assignments.
3
Add Tasks Manually
Hit ✚ Add Task in the header. Fill in the description, due date, time, priority, course, type, and any tags or notes. Priority is also auto-detected from keywords in the task name — words like "exam" or "final" will automatically flag as high priority.
4
Explore the Dashboard & Week View
The Dashboard shows your upcoming tasks ranked by urgency and a past-due section you can expand any time. Switch to Week View to see a full 7-day calendar — click any day to drill into exactly what's due. Use the arrow buttons to move forward and backward through weeks.
5
Review Courses & Track Progress
The Courses tab groups every task by detected course code. Each course card shows total, pending, and completed assignments along with a visual progress bar. Click a course card to filter the full task list down to just that course.
6
Ask the AI Assistant
Head to the 🤖 AI Assistant tab. Use the Quick Questions on the right for instant answers, or type anything in the chat — like "schedule my tasks this week", "what's due tomorrow?", or "which course has the most work?". The AI has full visibility into your tasks and will give you a real, personalized answer.
7
Focus with the Study Timer
Go to the ⏱ Study Timer tab. Pick a task from the list on the right to associate with your session, then choose a preset duration (25, 50, or 5-minute break) or enter a custom number of minutes. Hit Start and the animated ring timer will count you down. Stay in the zone.
8
Export & Manage Your Data
In ⚙ Settings you can see all your active auto-refresh sources, view task counts, and export your data. Export CSV gives a clean spreadsheet with Task, Date, Time, Type, Priority, Status, and Course columns — no clutter. Export JSON gives a full data backup. You can also clear all tasks from this panel if you want a fresh start.
